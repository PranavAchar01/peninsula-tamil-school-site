About Peninsula Tamil School

Preserving Tamil Heritage Through Education

Our Story

Peninsula Tamil School was established over 17 years ago as part of the Peninsula Indian Cultural Association (PICA) to serve the Tamil-speaking community in the San Francisco Bay Area. What began as a small group of families committed to passing on their linguistic and cultural heritage has grown into a thriving educational institution serving hundreds of students each year.

Our school operates on a volunteer-driven model, with dedicated parents, educators, and community members contributing their time and expertise to ensure that future generations maintain a strong connection to Tamil language, literature, and culture. This grassroots approach has allowed us to remain responsive to community needs while keeping our programs accessible and affordable.

Located at Hillsdale High School in San Mateo, we conduct classes every Sunday morning, providing a consistent and structured learning environment for students from Pre-Kindergarten through Grade 10, as well as adult learners seeking to improve their Tamil proficiency or reconnect with their heritage.

Our Mission

To provide quality Tamil language education and foster appreciation for Indian cultural heritage among children and adults in the Peninsula area through dedicated volunteer instruction and community engagement.

Our Vision

To be the leading Tamil educational institution in the Bay Area, empowering students with language proficiency and cultural knowledge that strengthens their identity and connects them to the global Tamil community.

What We Offer

  • Structured Curriculum: Age-appropriate lessons from Pre-K through Grade 10
  • Experienced Volunteer Teachers: Dedicated instructors with Tamil language expertise
  • Cultural Integration: Language learning combined with cultural education
  • Community Connection: Regular events fostering bonds among Tamil families
  • Accessible Location: Convenient Sunday classes at Hillsdale High School
  • Annual Celebration: Showcase student learning and cultural performances
  • Family Involvement: Opportunities for parents to participate in education
  • Nonprofit Model: Affordable fees supporting quality education
